What poses a THREAT to these endangered animals?


There are many threats that fuel the extinction of animals, some of these are:

HABITAT DESTRUCTION:

Deforestation, urbanization, and agriculture lead to the loss of natural habitats. Habitat destruction is one of the most
significant threats to wildlife, as it directly eliminates the places where animals live, breed, and find food.
Deforestation, driven by logging, agriculture, and development, removes vast tracts of forests, which are home to countless species.
Urbanization encroaches on natural areas, replacing them with cities, roads, and infrastructure.
Agricultural expansion often involves clearing land for crops and livestock, leading to habitat fragmentation,
which isolates animal populations and makes it difficult for them to find mates and resources.




CLIMATE CHANGE & POLLUTION:

Changes in climate can alter habitats and make them unsuitable for certain species. Climate change, driven by the
accumulation of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, leads to global temperature increases, changing precipitation patterns,
and more frequent extreme weather events. These changes can alter habitats in several ways: rising temperatures may
push species out of their thermal tolerance zones, melting ice caps reduce the habitat available for polar species,
and shifting weather patterns can affect the availability of food and water.
Contaminants in air, water, and soil can harm wildlife. Pollution takes many forms, including chemical pollutants,
plastics, and noise pollution. Chemical pollutants, such as pesticides, heavy metals, and industrial chemicals,
can poison wildlife, disrupt endocrine systems, and cause reproductive failures.
Water pollution from agricultural runoff, sewage, and industrial discharges can create dead zones in aquatic environments,
where oxygen levels are too low to support most life forms.




POACHING AND ILLEGAL TRADES:

Many species are threatened by illegal hunting and trade. Poaching, the illegal killing of wildlife, is a major threat to many endangered species.
Animals are often hunted for their valuable parts, such as elephant tusks, rhino horns, tiger bones, and pangolin scales,
which are used in traditional medicine, ornaments, and as status symbols. The illegal wildlife trade is a multi-billion-dollar industry
that operates globally, driving many species to the brink of extinction. Poaching disrupts population dynamics, reduces genetic diversity,
and can lead to the collapse of populations.
