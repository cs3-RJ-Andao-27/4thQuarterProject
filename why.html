Why must we SAVE endangered animals?


This importance comes down to many factors one of which is

BIODIVERSITY:

Endangered animals contribute to the diversity of life on Earth,
which is essential for resilient ecosystems. Biodiversity, the variety of life in all its forms and levels,
from genes to ecosystems, is crucial for the stability and resilience of natural systems.
High biodiversity ensures that ecosystems can recover from disturbances, such as natural disasters
or human activities, and continue to provide essential services. Each species plays a unique role in its ecosystem,
and the loss of one species can have cascading effects on others, potentially leading to the collapse of entire ecosystems.




GENETIC DIVERSITY:

Preserving various species maintains genetic diversity, which can be crucial for future research and biotechnology.
Genetic diversity within and between species provides the raw material for evolution and adaptation to changing environments.
It is also a reservoir of genetic resources that can be used for developing new medicines, improving agricultural crops, and
solving other scientific and technological challenges.




CULTURAL AND ETHICAL VALUES:

Many cultures value wildlife for their heritage and aesthetic reasons, and there's a moral obligation to protect other species from extinction.
Wildlife is an integral part of many cultural identities and traditions, contributing to art, folklore, and religious practices.
The presence of diverse animal species enriches human experiences, providing opportunities for recreation, education, and inspiration.
Ethically, many people believe that all species have an intrinsic right to exist and that humans have a responsibility to protect them from harm,
especially when human activities are the primary cause of their endangerment.
